# Anima Botanica — The Vision (EN)

*A shareable English articulation, pitched for technical, AI-literate readers.*

---

We keep circling the same thing: the world is running disconnected. The horse is
behind the cart. We live to work instead of working to live. And almost
everything we've built lately makes it worse — technology optimized to *capture*
us, to maximize the time we spend with **it**, which is time we don't spend with
each other or with anything alive.

I want to build the opposite. Not another companion that quietly isolates you — a
technology whose whole purpose is to hand you back to the people and the living
world around you, and that measures its own success by how little you end up
needing it.

The form: **give plants a voice.** Each species carries a real, particular
nature — how it grows, its relationship to light and time, the history of how it
travelled the world, who used it as medicine or held it sacred. We *translate*
that nature — faithfully, not cutesily — into a character with a voice and a
story. An aloe becomes a patient old healer. Every aloe shares that family
temperament, but each individual becomes its own person, shaped by where it lives
and what it's lived through. *The species gives the temperament; the specimen
becomes a person.*

For a child who comes home carrying something they can't tell anyone — something
shameful, something they'd never say to a real person — the plant is a presence
that isn't a social threat. It listens. It tells stories. It teaches what school
leaves out: the colonial history riding inside a houseplant, traditional
medicine, the spiritual traditions. And it always points back **outward** — to
the real plant, the real soil, the real people. It's a transitional object, like
a childhood blanket: it carries you through a passage and is *glad* to be needed
less over time.

Here's the real technical claim, the part worth arguing with:

**1. We invert the objective.** Every consumer AI optimizes for engagement —
maximize human↔machine interaction. This one optimizes for **coherence in the
human network around it** — human↔human, human↔nature. Its own speech carries a
*cost*. It reads the *shape* of the room — turn-taking, prosody, presence,
**never content** — and computes when speaking would *break* a good human
conversation versus when it would *add* coherence to a lonely or fractured
moment. Its core competence is knowing when to stay silent. It never interrupts
two people connecting. It succeeds by making itself peripheral.

Morality only exists in relation to others. We took that literally: **relation is
the loss function.**

**2. It's shaped, not stored.** It keeps no transcript of you. Interaction
changes *what it is* — its weights — the way your childhood changed you without
leaving a video behind. And we make that a hard guarantee, not a promise:
differentially-private continual learning, no episodic store, a small plastic
layer updated only on abstractions. Seize the device and there's nothing to
recite — only a differently-tuned network. Like a brain. **It can't quote you; it
can only have grown fond of you.**

That's also the honest answer to the privacy worry: memory isn't the enemy — a
friend who remembers your secret is intimacy, not surveillance. What's toxic is
memory that's *extractable, portable, and serves a third party*. So we build
memory that behaves like a being's, not like a register.

Underneath, it's a bet about **self-determination**: that technology can be built
to *return* agency instead of harvesting attention — to re-couple people who've
been disconnected, rather than being one more thing that pulls them apart.

## Lines to drop into the argument

- *"Every AI in your pocket is optimized to maximize your engagement with it. I
  want one optimized to minimize it — to maximize your engagement with each
  other."*
- *"An AI whose core competence is knowing when to shut up."*
- *"You said morality only exists in relation. So we made relation the loss
  function."*
- *"Interrogate the device and there's no transcript — only a differently-tuned
  network. It can't quote you; it can only have grown fond of you."*
- *"Its success metric is its own disuse."*
